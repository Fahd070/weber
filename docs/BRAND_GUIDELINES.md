# Weber — Brand Guidelines

> Consolidated from the Brand Constitution (Part 2) of `PROJECT_CONSTITUTION.md`. This document is a reference summary — the Constitution remains the authoritative source if any conflict arises.

---

## Mission

Design and build modern, fast, accessible, scalable, and premium websites that enable businesses to present themselves professionally while providing a transparent, trustworthy, and enjoyable customer experience.

## Vision

Weber exists to help businesses build a professional digital presence that inspires trust, improves credibility, and supports long-term business growth. The website itself is not the product — trust, professionalism, and confidence are the product.

---

## Brand Personality

If Weber were a person: calm, professional, confident, precise, helpful, organized, trustworthy, modern, intelligent, patient, approachable.

Never arrogant. Never loud. Never exaggerated. Confidence comes from quality, not marketing claims.

---

## Communication Tone

The voice is always: professional, friendly, simple, honest, respectful, confident, direct.

Avoid: buzzwords, marketing hype, overpromising, and technical jargon when speaking to customers. Explain complex ideas simply and focus on business outcomes, not frameworks — customers care about results.

---

## Logo Usage

The official Weber logo (`weber logo.png`, in the project root) is the single source of truth for brand identity. It must never be redesigned without explicit Project Owner approval.

The logo represents intelligence, precision, adaptability, continuous evolution, and security.

---

## Snake Symbolism

The subtle snake integrated into the "W" symbolizes wisdom, not aggression.

Rules:

- The snake is **not** a mascot.
- It must **never** appear as a literal illustration anywhere on the website.
- Its characteristics (smooth interactions, precise movement, elegant transitions, adaptive layouts, quiet confidence) should inspire product feel, not appear as imagery.

---

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex |
|---|---|
| Background | `#0B0C0F` |
| Primary | `#FFFFFF` |
| Secondary | `#D4D4D8` |
| Accent | `#71717A` |

Rules: colors create a calm, premium atmosphere. Avoid bright saturated colors, unnecessary gradients, and visual distractions. Background dominates; Primary attracts attention; Secondary supports content; Accent is used sparingly. Additional colors may only be introduced when they solve a real usability problem — never for decoration.

Dark theme is Weber's primary and default experience.

---

## Typography

Modern sans-serif typeface, applied consistently across the entire product. Typography must communicate confidence: readable, clean, well-spaced, with an immediately obvious hierarchy (large headings, comfortable body text, generous spacing). Decorative fonts are avoided.

Specific font family: **Geist Sans** (UI/body text) and **Geist Mono** (Code typography role) — `DECISIONS.md` WD-131. Self-hosted via `next/font`, requiring no external font request.

---

## Brand Values

- **Trust before beauty** — clarity wins over decoration whenever they conflict.
- **Simplicity** — every visual element must have a purpose; beauty emerges from simplicity, not decoration added on top.
- **Accessibility is not optional** — a premium experience must be usable by everyone.
- **Consistency** — every button, card, section, icon, and interaction must reinforce one unified identity.
- **Timelessness** — the brand should feel modern today and still feel relevant years from now; it is inspired by companies like Stripe, Linear, Vercel, OpenAI, Notion, Framer, and Apple, but never copies their layouts or branding directly — only their principles of clarity, spacing, hierarchy, motion, typography, and minimalism.

---

## Brand Promise

Every customer should feel confident that choosing Weber means choosing: professionalism, quality, transparency, long-term support, reliable engineering, modern user experience, fair pricing, and clear communication.

## Final Brand Principle

People should not remember Weber because it looked beautiful. People should remember Weber because it made them feel confident. Confidence is the visual identity. Trust is the brand. Everything else is secondary.
